"A Cabin on the Coast" is a science fantasy short story by American writer Gene Wolfe, initially published in the February 1984 issue of The Magazine of Fantasy & Science Fiction and collected in Endangered Species (1989) and The Best Of Gene Wolfe (2009).ISFDB entry It was nominated for the 1985 Nebula Award for Best Short Story as well as the 1985 Locus award.The LOCUS Index to SF Awards ==Plot summary== The story follows Timothy Ryan Neal, the son of a famous politician. When he takes his girlfriend to his father’s beach house (or cabin) he wakes up one morning to find her missing. Though the police suggest that she drowned in the ocean, Timothy is convinced that she was kidnapped by the mysterious ship he sees now and again in the sea. Concerned not only about her life, but also about his father’s reputation, he journeys out to try to make a deal with the ship. ==References== Category:Science fiction short stories Category:1984 short stories Category:Short stories by Gene Wolfe Category:Works originally published in The Magazine of Fantasy & Science Fiction
